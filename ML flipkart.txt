PS1 Winning Strategy: Parking-Induced Congestion

Considering:

Only 6 days left
You already have the dataset
Judges are from Flipkart + Bengaluru Traffic Police
They want a working prototype, not a research paper

I would not build a complicated deep learning model.

I would build a system that answers one question:

"Where should Bengaluru Traffic Police deploy enforcement teams tomorrow to reduce congestion caused by illegal parking?"

That directly matches the problem statement.

Understanding the Actual Problem

Most teams will build:

Illegal parking count dashboard

That is NOT enough.

The problem statement says:

quantify impact on traffic flow and prioritize enforcement zones

Therefore your solution should be:

Violation Data
        ↓
Hotspot Detection
        ↓
Congestion Impact Score
        ↓
Enforcement Recommendation
        ↓
Interactive Dashboard
What I Think Judges Want

Police officers don't care that:

Location A = 500 violations

They care:

Location A causes severe congestion
Location B causes moderate congestion

Send officers to A first.

Therefore your output should be:

Priority Ranking
Rank	Area	Risk Score	Recommendation
1	Shivajinagar	95	Deploy 3 officers
2	Upparpet	92	Deploy towing vehicle
3	City Market	89	Deploy 2 officers

This is useful.

Step 3: Hotspot Detection

This is where many teams stop.

Use:

DBSCAN

Cluster parking violations geographically.

DBSCAN

Output:

Hotspot 1
Hotspot 2
Hotspot 3
...

Example:

Commercial Street
MG Road
Majestic
Shivajinagar

Why DBSCAN?

Because:

no need to specify clusters
finds dense regions
works perfectly for hotspot detection

Judges love this.

Step 4: Congestion Impact Score

This is the most important part.

Create your own score.

Example:

Impact Score =
0.4 × Violation Frequency
+
0.2 × Peak Hour Violations
+
0.2 × Vehicle Diversity
+
0.2 × Recency Trend

Normalize everything.

Final score:

0-100

Example:

Area	Score
Shivajinagar	96
Upparpet	91
City Market	88

Now you have:

Congestion Impact Quantification

Exactly what judges asked.

Step 5: Forecast Future Hotspots

Very easy model.

Do not waste time with LSTM.

Use:

XGBoost

or

Random Forest

Input:

hour
day
location
vehicle type
historical violations

Output:

Expected violations tomorrow

Example:

Shivajinagar
Tomorrow 6-8 PM

Expected violations:
47

Now your system becomes predictive.
