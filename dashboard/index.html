Congestion Impact Score (CIS) Formula

The CIS is a weighted composite score (0-100) calculated per hotspot zone:

  • 30% - Violation Density: Spatially normalized frequency of violations.
  • 25% - Peak-Hour Concentration: Share of violations during rush hours.
  • 20% - Junction Proximity: Penalizes hotspots chokepointing major intersections.
  • 15% - Road Type Weight: Ring/Main roads get 100%, Flyovers 80%, side streets 40%.
  • 10% - Vehicle Mix: Higher penalty for heavy bus/commercial transport presence.
Carriageway Capacity Loss Estimation

Estimates physical lane blockage from illegal parking:

  • Each parked vehicle blocks ~3.2m of lane width.
  • On a 2-lane road (e.g. Cottonpet), a single parked vehicle causes a ~33% capacity reduction.
  • On a 4-lane road (e.g. ORR), blockage is ~17%.
  • A 1.5× congestion multiplier is applied to peak hour volumes to account for compound delay & spillover.

Greedy Resource Dispatch Algorithm

Allocates scarce BTP officers & towing units to maximize city-wide violation prevention:

  • 1. Sort: Prioritizes all 539 zones by predicted next-day risk score.
  • 2. Match: Deploys resources based on risk band thresholds:
    • Very High: 3 Officers + 1 Tow Vehicle (Barricade Support)
    • High: 2 Officers (Active Patrols)
    • Medium: 1 Officer (Mobile Monitoring)
  • 3. Constraint: Halts allocations once available officer/tow counts reach zero.
Comparative Simulation Baselines

We benchmark our AI-Guided plan against two traditional strategies:

  • AI-Guided Plan: Achieves targeted coverage by placing personnel in predicted hotspots for the specific tomorrow watch windows.
  • Historical Plan: Deploys static resources based on historical monthly averages, failing to capture daily spikes and shifting temporal peaks.
  • Random Patrol: Distributes officers uniformly, representing a base reactive monitoring benchmark.

Model Explainability

Understanding what drives the model's predictions — feature importance and SHAP analysis

🧠 Why Should You Trust This Model?

The ensemble model's predictions are primarily driven by:

📈
Recent Violation Momentum

7-day rolling sums and peak-hour sums capture how active a zone has been in the past week.

📊
Rolling 7-Day & 14-Day Trends

Multi-window moving averages smooth out daily noise and highlight persistent congestion patterns.

🔥
Hotspot Persistence

Lag counts at 14 days confirm zones that are chronically problematic, not just one-day spikes.

🏗️
Zone Structure & Diversity

Number of unique locations and junctions within a zone indicate its structural complexity and multi-source violations.

⏱️
Lagged Counts

Yesterday's and last week's violation counts serve as strong autoregressive signals for tomorrow's prediction.

💡 Key Takeaway

The final Weighted Average Ensemble of tuned LightGBM, XGBoost, and HistGradientBoosting achieves the highest Validation Daily Precision@10 of 68.7%, meaning nearly 7 out of 10 zones flagged for deployment each day are genuinely in the true top-10.
